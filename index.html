IN-CAMPUS

Camarines Norte State College (CNSC) College of Education Laboratory School, also known as the Abano Campus, is located at J. Lukban Extension, Daet, Camarines Norte. It began as the Abao Pilot Elementary School in 1953 and became a regional pilot school in 1959 due to its remarkable performance. The land was generously donated by Dr. Jose Abao, whose legacy continues to inspire learners. The campus is currently headed by Ma'am Judelin S. Alvarez, who continues to uphold the school's commitment to providing quality education. Today, the campus serves both Elementary and Secondary Laboratory Schools, catering to regular students and learners with special needs. With facilities and beneficial resources the campus provides an environment that supports effective teaching and meaningful learning.

During my Field Study at CNSC-Abano, I experienced moments that shaped me as a student and future educator. Observing classrooms, learners, and my resource teacher allowed me to connect theory to real teaching practices. Working alongside fellow pre-service teachers offered opportunities to share insights and learn collaboratively. Each day offered new meaningful insight and lessons. Watching teachers plan and deliver lessons while students responded with curiosity showed how small adjustments in strategies can greatly influence learning. Patience, creativity, and empathy were clearly essential in making lessons effective and enjoyable.

Overall, my experience at CNSC-Abano was inspiring and transformative. It demonstrated that education is not only about academics but also about guiding and supporting young minds. The lessons learned, connections made, and growth experienced have prepared me to face future challenges and strengthened my commitment to becoming a teacher who makes a positive and lasting impact on students' lives.
